1. Background¶

Customer churn is a term used especially in the world of subscription based businesses to describe loss of customers.

Churn is important because it directly affects your service's profitability. It is common to assume that the profitability of a service is directly related to the growth of its customer base. That might lead business owners to imply that in order to grow their customer base, the rate of acquiring new customers must exceed the churn rate.

In order to answers the questions "Are we losing customers?" and "If so, how?", our team made a Churn Analysis to support the Management to take action.

Based on these box plots, we can find:

  • For the "gender", the distribution is more or less the same.
  • If the client is a senior citizen, his/her monthly charges are more likely to be higher than the one that is not a senior citizen, and the distribution is more concentrated.
  • If the client has partnerships/dependents with the company, his/her monthly charges are likely to be slightly higher than the ones that does not have a partnership/dependents.
  • For the clients that are subscriding to phone services, their monthly charges could be in a larger range than the ones that don't, and is more likely to have a higher charge.
  • The feature "MultipleLines" is directly related to "PhoneService", so within the one that has phone services, the one that has multiple lines is likely to be charged more than the one that doesn't.
  • When we are looking at the monthly charges' distribution of the "InternetService", it turns out that if the client does not suscribes to internet service, then his/her monthly charges is likely to be very low. Moreover, within the one that had internet services, the one that has fiber optic is charged more than the one that has DSL. For the feature "OnlineSecurity", "OnlineBackup", "DeviceProtection", "TechSupport", "StreamingTV", and "StreamingMovies", they are dependent on "InternetService". Except for "OnlineBackup", the other five feature with "Yes" has higher monthly charges than "No"s.
  • As for the contract type of the customer, we observe that for one year and two year ocntract, the ranges of the charges are larger, when the median tend to be similar.
  • If a client is paying his/her bills paperlessly, the monthly charges tend to be higher than the one who doesn't.
  • Finally, within the one who has paperless billing, the payment method with bank transfer or credit card, the range of their monthly charge tend to be larger, while the one with electronic check is likely to be charged more.

    active_customers_data[cate] = active_customers_data[cate].apply(lambda x: 1 if x=='Yes' else 0)

# for the rest features, we use one-hot code
def dummies(col,df):
    dummies = pd.get_dummies(df[col], prefix=col)
    df = pd.concat([df, dummies], axis=1)
    df = df.drop(columns=col)
    return df

active_customers_data = dummies('InternetService', active_customers_data)
active_customers_data = dummies('Contract', active_customers_data)
