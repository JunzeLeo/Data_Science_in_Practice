1. Background¶

Customer churn is a term used especially in the world of subscription based businesses to describe loss of customers.

Churn is important because it directly affects your service's profitability. It is common to assume that the profitability of a service is directly related to the growth of its customer base. That might lead business owners to imply that in order to grow their customer base, the rate of acquiring new customers must exceed the churn rate.

In order to answers the questions "Are we losing customers?" and "If so, how?", our team made a Churn Analysis to support the Management to take action.

    dummies = pd.get_dummies(df[col], prefix=col)
    df = pd.concat([df, dummies], axis=1)
    df = df.drop(columns=col)
    return df

def plot_confusion_matrix(confusion_matrix):
    df_cm = pd.DataFrame(confusion_matrix, index=['Churn','No Churn'], columns=['Churn','No Churn'])
    fig = plt.figure(figsize=(7,5))
    heatmap = sns.heatmap(df_cm, annot=True, fmt="d", cmap='Greens')
    heatmap.yaxis.set_ticklabels(heatmap.yaxis.get_ticklabels(), rotation=0, ha='right', fontsize=12)
    heatmap.xaxis.set_ticklabels(heatmap.xaxis.get_ticklabels(), rotation=0, ha='right', fontsize=12)
    plt.ylim(0, 2)
    plt.title('The confusion matrix of the predicted results')
    plt.xlabel('Predicted label')
    plt.ylabel('True label')
    
def plot_roc_curve(fpr, tpr):
    print('The area under ROC curve is {}.'.format(auc(fpr, tpr)))
    plt.figure(figsize=(6,6))
    plt.title('The ROC Curve of classifier')
    plt.plot(fpr, tpr, linewidth=2)
    plt.plot([0, 1], [0, 1], 'k--')
    plt.axis([-0.005, 1, 0, 1.005])
    plt.xticks(np.arange(0,1, 0.1))
    plt.xlabel("False Positive Rate")
    plt.ylabel("True Positive Rate")
